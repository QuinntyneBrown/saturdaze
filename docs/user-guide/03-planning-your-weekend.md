# 3. Planning Your Weekend

This is the part that does the magic. In this chapter we go from a
blank weekend to a complete plan in about a minute.

The home screen — labelled **This Weekend** in the app — is where it
all happens.

## Step 1: Open the app and read the room

When you open Saturdaze and land on **This Weekend**, the top of the
screen greets you and points out the weather:

- **The forecast** — a little strip showing Saturday and Sunday side by
  side, each with a high, low, and a short note like "Light breeze,
  perfect for outdoors" or "Cloudy by 2 pm — plan indoors after."

Glance at this first. It usually tells you whether you're looking at an
indoor or outdoor weekend before you even tap anything.

## Step 2: Tap "Plan This Weekend"

The big button on the home screen says **Plan This Weekend**. Tap it.

In a few seconds, you'll see two day cards appear — one for Saturday,
one for Sunday — each showing:

- The **date** and **weather**
- The **day's highlight** (e.g. "Lavender fields at Terre Bleu")
- A few small **chips** that summarize the day — for example:
  - 🔒 **9:00 swim** (something locked in)
  - 🚗 **45 min drive** (the longest drive of the day)
  - 🌿 **Outdoor day** or 🏠 **Indoor afternoon**

That's your weekend at a glance. If both cards look reasonable, you can
stop here — you have a plan.

## Step 3: Read the "Heads-up" panel

Below the day cards is a section called **A heads-up**. This is where
Saturdaze surfaces things you'd want to know but might miss:

- "Lavender bloom peaks this weekend. I tucked it into Saturday
  afternoon."
- "Sara mentioned a Costco run. Want me to slot it Sunday morning
  before church?"
- "Rain moves in after 3 pm Sunday."

Each note has a small action button — **See on the map**, **Add to
Sunday**, etc. Tap to act on it, ignore to dismiss.

## Step 4: Open a day to see the timeline

Tap either day card to open its full **Itinerary**. You'll see a
timeline running from morning through evening, broken into blocks:

- **Locked** blocks like swim, church, your workout
- **Activity** blocks like a hike, the Zoo, or the Rec Room
- **Meal** blocks for lunch and dinner
- **Drive** segments showing how long it takes to get there
- **Downtime** blocks — quiet time, kids recharge, you breathe

For everything you can do here, see
[The Itinerary](04-the-itinerary.md).

## Step 5: Use the Quick Actions

Back on the home screen, scroll down to **Quick actions**. You'll find
shortcuts for the most common moves:

- **Regenerate the weekend** — same commitments, fresh ideas. Useful
  when "it's fine" but not exciting.
- **Lock what's already perfect** — pin the blocks you love before
  regenerating, so they survive the shuffle.
- **Share this weekend** — generates a read-only preview link you can
  send to your partner via Messages, Mail, or any share target.

## What if you don't like the plan?

A few options, from smallest to biggest:

1. **Swap one block.** Open the day, tap the block you want to change,
   choose **Swap**. See [The Itinerary](04-the-itinerary.md).
2. **Regenerate one day.** From the day card, choose **Regenerate**.
   Only that day reshuffles; the other one stays as it is.
3. **Regenerate the whole weekend.** From Quick actions, tap
   **Regenerate the weekend**. Locked blocks stay locked; everything
   else is fair game.
4. **Lock the keepers first, then regenerate.** Often the best move —
   protect what works, then ask the app to redo the rest.

## A typical first-weekend flow

Here's what most families do the first time:

1. Open the app, see the forecast.
2. Tap **Plan This Weekend**.
3. Read both day cards. Like Saturday, unsure about Sunday.
4. Tap Sunday → **Regenerate**.
5. Get a better Sunday. Lock Saturday.
6. Tap **Share this weekend**.
7. Done.

Total time: under two minutes.
