# Saturdaze User Guide

Welcome! Saturdaze is a family weekend planner. You tap one button, and it
hands you back a ready-to-use Saturday and Sunday — with activities,
meals, drive times, and quiet moments — all built around the things your
family already does (swim lessons, church, bedtime, that workout you
squeeze in).

This guide walks you through the app the way you would actually use it,
step by step, in plain language. No tech talk.

## How to read this guide

If this is your first time, start with the first three chapters in order:

1. [Getting Started](01-getting-started.md) — What Saturdaze does and the

2. [Setting Up Your Family](02-setting-up-your-family.md) — A one-time

3. [Planning Your Weekend](03-planning-your-weekend.md) — How to go from
   "blank weekend" to "we have a plan" in one tap.

After that, dip into whichever chapter matches what you're trying to do:

4. [The Itinerary](04-the-itinerary.md) — Reading and tweaking the day,
   block by block.
5. [Finding Activities](05-finding-activities.md) — Browsing things to do

6. [Picking a Restaurant](06-picking-restaurants.md) — Settling the food
   debate without a fight.
7. [Events This Weekend](07-events-this-weekend.md) — Circuses, theatre,

8. [Errands and Shopping](08-errands-and-shopping.md) — Slotting in a

10. [Tips, Shortcuts, and Troubleshooting](10-tips-and-troubleshooting.md)
    — Little things that make the app feel like yours.

## Who Saturdaze is for

Saturdaze is built for families who:

- Have kids of mixed ages (think 5 to 12) and a 9 p.m. bedtime
- Live in or near Port Credit, Mississauga
- Already have fixed weekend commitments (swimming, church, a workout)
- Like parks, hikes, the Rec Room, the Zoo, live theatre, short drives,
  and lavender fields
- Want suggestions, not a blank calendar
- Find the "what's for lunch?" debate exhausting

If that sounds like your house, you're in the right place.

## A note on the screenshots

Throughout this guide, screen names match what you see at the top of each
page in the app — for example **This Weekend**, **Family Profile**, and
**Activities**. If a button or card is mentioned in **bold**, that's
exactly what its label says in the app.

When in doubt, the bottom of the app always has a small navigation bar
with the main sections. You can tap it any time to get back.
