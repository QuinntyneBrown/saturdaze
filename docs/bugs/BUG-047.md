**Status:** Fixed

## Summary

On iPhone, when the page is scrolled to the top of any signed-in screen,
mobile Safari's bottom chrome (URL/tab bar with the back/forward chevrons)
expands and overlaps the floating `sd-bottom-nav`. The four tabs and the
"Home / Plan / Saved / Profile" labels sit partly hidden behind the system
toolbar. Scrolling down again collapses Safari's chrome and the nav
reappears, but it should never be obscured.

The viewport meta tag does not opt into safe-area handling
(`viewport-fit=cover` is missing), and `sd-bottom-nav` is anchored at a
fixed `bottom: 12px` with no `env(safe-area-inset-bottom)` allowance. The
floating-pill design assumed a fully visible viewport bottom edge, which
isn't what iOS Safari gives you when the address bar is at its expanded
"top of page" height.

## Steps to reproduce

1. Open <https://gentle-rock-070070c0f.7.azurestaticapps.net/login> in
   mobile Safari on iPhone.
2. Sign in (any seeded account).
3. Land on `/weekend` (or any signed-in route with the bottom-nav).
4. Make sure the page is scrolled all the way to the top â€” Safari's
   bottom toolbar is at its tallest there.
5. Observe the bottom-nav: the system chrome overlaps the lower half of
   the pill, hiding labels and increasing the chance of mis-taps.

## Expected

- The bottom-nav is always fully visible above iOS Safari's bottom
  toolbar **and** above the home indicator on Face-ID iPhones.
- The `.sd-frame` content area reserves enough bottom padding for the
  nav plus the home-indicator inset, so scrolled-to-bottom content
  cannot disappear behind the nav either.

## Actual

- At scroll-top, Safari's expanded bottom toolbar overlaps the pill.
- On Face-ID iPhones (no Safari toolbar visible, e.g. in PWA mode), the
  pill sits on top of the home indicator instead of cleanly above it.

## Likely source

- `frontend/projects/saturdaze/src/index.html`
  (missing `viewport-fit=cover` on the viewport meta)
- `frontend/projects/components/src/lib/bottom-nav/bottom-nav.scss`
  (fixed `bottom: 12px`, no `env(safe-area-inset-bottom)` allowance)
- `frontend/projects/components/src/lib/styles/_global.scss`
  (`.sd-frame { padding-bottom: calc(var(--sd-nav-h) + 16px); }` â€”
  doesn't include the safe-area inset either)

## Acceptance

- iPhone Safari: at scroll-top, the entire bottom-nav pill is visible
  above the system toolbar â€” no part of the four tabs or their labels is
  occluded.
- iPhone Face-ID PWA: the bottom-nav clears the home indicator.
- Scrolled-to-end content on every signed-in route (`/weekend`,
  `/itinerary`, `/activities`, `/restaurants`, `/saved`, `/events`,
  `/profile`) remains visible above the nav â€” nothing hides behind it.
- A Playwright check or manual screenshot at 390Ă—844 with simulated
  safe-area insets confirms the nav's top edge is at least
  `safe-area-inset-bottom + 12px` from the bottom of the viewport.

## Notes

The same fix (`env(safe-area-inset-bottom)`) covers two related problems
in one stroke: hiding behind Safari's bottom toolbar at scroll-top, and
hiding behind the iPhone home indicator on notch / Dynamic Island
devices. `viewport-fit=cover` is required for `env(safe-area-inset-*)`
to ever resolve to non-zero values.
